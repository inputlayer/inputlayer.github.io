InputLayer + Vector Databases

Your vector database finds content that looks like your query. InputLayer follows chains of facts to derive conclusions. Most real applications need both.

A shopper asks "ink for my printer." Every cartridge in the catalog is semantically similar to "printer ink" - a Canon PG-245 and an Epson 202 are nearly identical in embedding space. But only one fits the printer the shopper bought eight months ago. That connection - purchase history to compatibility matrix to live inventory - is a chain of structured facts, not a similarity match.

InputLayer traces that chain in a single query and keeps the answer current as inventory changes.

Three things InputLayer handles that vector databases weren't built for

Following multi-hop chains. When the answer requires tracing relationships across separate data sources - supply chain dependencies, corporate ownership structures, medication interactions - InputLayer follows the chain automatically. You define the relationships as rules. The engine walks them to any depth.

Port disruption reported
which suppliers ship through this port?
Supplier A, Supplier C
which components do they provide?
Component X, Component Y
which products use those components?
Product Alpha, Product Beta
InputLayer traced the chain from disruption to affected products

Evaluating access policies at query time. When permissions involve hierarchies - managers see their reports' documents, and their reports' reports, and so on - InputLayer resolves the full chain as part of the search. Results come back already filtered for what the user is allowed to see.

VP of Engineering searches for "deployment best practices"
InputLayer resolves authorization
Walk org hierarchy: 36 people in reporting chain
filter documents
847 documents from authorized authors
rank by similarity
Top 10 results, all authorized
One query, one pass. Authorization and search together.

Keeping conclusions current. When someone changes roles, their permissions update in the next query. When a supplier is reinstated, blocked orders unblock automatically. When a product goes out of stock, recommendations retract in milliseconds. InputLayer tracks which conclusions depend on which facts and updates only what's affected.

How teams use them together

The most common pattern: your vector database handles straightforward similarity search, and InputLayer handles queries that need reasoning. "Find similar documents" goes to the vector DB. "Find documents similar to X that this user is authorized to see through their reporting chain" goes to InputLayer, which combines the authorization logic with vector search in a single pass.

Some teams use both in parallel. Others move their reasoning-heavy queries entirely to InputLayer and keep the vector DB for high-throughput similarity at scale.

Performance

InputLayer's incremental computation means that when facts change, only affected conclusions recompute. On a 2,000-node graph with 400,000 derived relationships, a single fact change takes 6.83ms - not the 11.3 seconds a full recompute would take. That's 1,652x faster.

For pure vector similarity at massive scale (billions of vectors), dedicated vector databases are purpose-built and optimized for that specific workload.
